# Books worth reading

> For depth that doesn't fit in a paper.

## AI engineering as a discipline

- **Chip Huyen.** *AI Engineering.* O'Reilly; 2025. ISBN 978-1098166304. The canonical "AI engineering" book of the LLM era.
- **Chip Huyen.** *Designing Machine Learning Systems.* O'Reilly; 2022. ISBN 978-1098107963. The MLOps prequel to the above.
- **Andriy Burkov.** *The Hundred-Page Machine Learning Book.* 2019. ISBN 978-1999579500. Compact ML refresher.
- **Lakshmanan, Robinson, Munn.** *Machine Learning Design Patterns.* O'Reilly; 2020. ISBN 978-1098115784.

## Foundations

- **Christopher Bishop.** *Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning.* Springer; 2006. ISBN 978-0387310732. Classical reference.
- **Kevin P. Murphy.** *Probabilistic Machine Learning: An Introduction* / *Advanced Topics.* MIT Press; 2022–2023. ISBN 978-0262046824 / 978-0262048439. Modern, encyclopedic.
- **Ian Goodfellow, Yoshua Bengio, Aaron Courville.** *Deep Learning.* MIT Press; 2016. ISBN 978-0262035613. The deep learning textbook.
- **Gilbert Strang.** *Linear Algebra and Learning from Data.* Wellesley-Cambridge; 2019. ISBN 978-0692196380.
- **Cover & Thomas.** *Elements of Information Theory.* 2nd ed. Wiley; 2006. ISBN 978-0471241959.

## LLMs and transformers

- **Jay Alammar & Maarten Grootendorst.** *Hands-On Large Language Models.* O'Reilly; 2024. ISBN 978-1098150969.
- **Sebastian Raschka.** *Build a Large Language Model (From Scratch).* Manning; 2024. ISBN 978-1633437166. Exactly what it says.
- **Lewis Tunstall, Leandro von Werra, Thomas Wolf.** *Natural Language Processing with Transformers.* O'Reilly; 2022. ISBN 978-1098103248.

## Distributed systems and infra

- **Martin Kleppmann.** *Designing Data-Intensive Applications.* O'Reilly; 2017. ISBN 978-1449373320. The systems book.
- **Patterson, Beyer, et al.** *Site Reliability Engineering.* Google / O'Reilly; 2016. ISBN 978-1491929124.
- **Reis & Housley.** *Fundamentals of Data Engineering.* O'Reilly; 2022. ISBN 978-1098108304.

## RL and alignment

- **Sutton & Barto.** *Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction.* 2nd ed. MIT Press; 2018. ISBN 978-0262039246. The reference.
- **Stuart Russell.** *Human Compatible.* Viking; 2019. ISBN 978-0525558613. AI safety framing.

## Engineering culture

- **Will Larson.** *Staff Engineer.* 2021. The senior IC track.
- **Forsgren, Humble, Kim.** *Accelerate.* IT Revolution; 2018. ISBN 978-1942788331. DORA metrics.
- **Camille Fournier.** *The Manager's Path.* O'Reilly; 2017. ISBN 978-1491973899. For when you mentor.

## Python / engineering

- **Luciano Ramalho.** *Fluent Python.* 2nd ed. O'Reilly; 2022. ISBN 978-1492056355.
- **Brett Slatkin.** *Effective Python.* 2nd ed. Addison-Wesley; 2019. ISBN 978-0134853987.

## Worth reading even though they're old

- **Brooks.** *The Mythical Man-Month.* 1975. Software projects in general; ages well.
- **Hunt & Thomas.** *The Pragmatic Programmer.* 1999. Engineering values.

## Reading cadence

A reasonable rhythm: read one book per quarter cover-to-cover, three or four in parallel as references. The compounding from steady reading dwarfs almost any other professional-development investment.
